### **Traits**

**Undead Fortitude.** If the goliath is reduced to 0 hit points, it must make a Constitution saving throw with a DC of **5 + the damage taken**, unless the damage is radiant or from a critical hit. On a success, the goliath drops to **1 hit point** instead.

**Stone-Bound Frame.** The goliath has advantage on Strength and Constitution saving throws, and it cannot be knocked prone while it is conscious.

**Bulwark of Bone.** Allies of Orren within 10 feet of the goliath gain **half cover** (+2 AC and Dexterity saving throws) while the goliath is conscious.

**Relentless Advance.** Difficult terrain doesn’t cost the goliath extra movement.

---

### **Actions**

**Multiattack.** The goliath makes **two attacks** with its bone maul.

**Bone Maul.**  
_Melee Weapon Attack:_ +8 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target.  
_Hit:_ **15 (2d8 + 6) bludgeoning damage.**

**Grave Slam.** _(Recharge 5–6)_  
The goliath smashes the ground. Each creature of the goliath’s choice within **10 feet** must succeed on a **DC 15 Strength saving throw** or take **18 (4d8)** bludgeoning damage and be knocked **prone**. On a success, the creature takes half damage and isn’t knocked prone.

---

### **Reaction**

**Intercept Blow.**  
When a creature the goliath can see within 5 feet is hit by an attack, the goliath can impose disadvantage on that attack roll.

---

### **Description**

These towering undead are the skeletal remains of goliath warriors, reforged with necromantic stone-binding rituals. Their bones are etched with runes of endurance, anchoring them to the battlefield like living ramparts. They do not rage, do not flee, and do not fall easily—serving as Orren’s immovable guardians and shock absorbers.
